Dreadnaught - Captain/Support

Wade Penfold

Wade "Dreadnaught" Penfold is 21 years old and from Wyoming. He first started playing Nintendo games when he was around 4 years old. Some of his favorites were Super Mario Brothers and Paperboy. He later received a N64 and loved games like Banjo Kazooie and Super Smash Brothers. Wade moved to a campground for a summer where he played copious amounts of Tekken 2 and moved on to first truly realize competitive games when he moved on to Halo 2. From there he progressed to WoW, from Wrath to Cataclysm and secured an exceptional arena rating. He also achieved diamond league in Starcraft 2 despite not even playing it often. Wade started League of Legends around the time the champion "Vayne" came out and got to platinum for both seasons 2 and 3. He now joins Heroes of the Storm as a deadly support and one of the team's fiercest competitors.

Glaurung - Shot Caller/Warrior

Mike Fisk

Mike "Glaurung" Fisk is the melee assassin and head shot caller for Tempo Storm. At age 14, Fisk started competing in tournaments for Starcraft 2 and achieved the rank of Grandmaster. After his swap to League of Legends, he got to diamond 1 in a short amount of time and has now fallen in love with Heroes, making a name for himself as one of the most aggressive players in the game.

Arthelon - Carry

Taylor Eder

Taylor "Arthelon" Eder is a true veteran in the moba genre beginning with the ever-popular League of Legends in which he achieved Platinum rank during season 1 which was the highest rank at the time. From there he continued to season 2 and ranked top 3 on the North American ranked ladder, convincingly proving his merit. He competed in the Challenger League in Seasons 3 and 4 and his team took 1st place at MLG Anaheim 2013. Taylor has found a new passion in Heroes due to its refreshing take on the genre. For Arthelon, Heroes has blown League out of the water.

So1dier - Flex

Josh Miller

Josh "So1dier" Miller started his esports career way back in the Halo 2 days where he took top 10 in the 1v1 leaderboards. He would go on to take 1st place in multiple tournaments, both local and state-wide, for Halo 1 and 2. Josh competed in the MLG circuit for a brief time when Shadowrun for Xbox 360 was on the circuit taking 3nd and 4th place. After swapping to League of Legends, he was a part of the top ranked 5's team, Rev Gaming, getting Diamond rank in solo queue, 3's, and 5's queue in season 2. He has grown to be one of the most respected players in the heroes scene with his impressively versatile hero pool and informative stream.
